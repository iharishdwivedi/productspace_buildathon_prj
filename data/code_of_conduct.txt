EMPLOYEE CODE OF CONDUCT AND ETHICS

1. Introduction: Statement of commitment to integrity, ethical behavior, and professional standards.
2. Scope: This code applies to all employees, contractors, and interns at all times (in-office, remote, or at client sites).
3. Compliance with Laws: All employees must obey all applicable laws and regulations in the regions they operate.
4. Anti-Harassment & Bullying: Zero-tolerance policy for harassment (sexual, verbal, physical) or bullying in any form.
5. Equal Opportunity: Commitment to diversity and inclusion; no discrimination based on race, gender, religion, age, disability, or orientation.
6. Professionalism: Maintain a respectful, collaborative, and professional demeanor with colleagues, clients, and partners.
7. Conflict of Interest: Employees must avoid any personal, financial, or other interests that could conflict with their duties to the company.
8. Disclosing Conflicts: Requirement to immediately disclose any potential or actual conflicts of interest to HR.
9. Confidentiality (Company): Do not disclose non-public company information (e.g., financial data, roadmaps, internal strategies).
10. Confidentiality (Client): Client data and project details are strictly confidential and must not be shared outside the approved team.
11. Confidentiality (Employee): Employee personal data (salaries, medical info) must be protected and handled only by authorized HR personnel.
12. Protection of Company Assets: Use company property (laptops, software, facilities) responsibly and for legitimate business purposes.
13. Intellectual Property (IP): All work, code, and inventions created by an employee during employment are the exclusive property of the company.
14. Insider Trading: Prohibits trading company or client stock based on non-public information.
15. Gifts and Entertainment: Guidelines on accepting or giving gifts; modest gifts are acceptable, but bribes (cash, high-value items) are forbidden.
16. Data Privacy: Adhere to all data privacy laws (e.g., GDPR, CCPA) when handling customer or user data.
17. Acceptable Use Policy (AUP): Governs the use of company internet, email, and systems; no illegal, offensive, or non-business high-bandwidth use.
18. Social Media: Guidelines for personal social media; do not represent personal opinions as company policy; do not disclose confidential info.
19. Dress Code: Maintain a professional, "business casual" appearance, especially during client video calls or in-office days.
20. Alcohol and Substance Abuse: Prohibits the use, possession, or influence of illegal drugs or alcohol during work hours or on company premises.
21. Moonlighting / Outside Work: Any external employment (e.g., "second job," "freelancing") must be disclosed and pre-approved to avoid conflicts.
22. Whistleblower Policy: Protection for employees who report illegal or unethical conduct in good faith.
23. Reporting Violations: Clear process for reporting Code of Conduct violations (e.g., to manager, HR, or anonymous ethics hotline).
24. No Retaliation: The company strictly prohibits retaliation against anyone who reports a concern.
25. Investigation Process: All reported violations will be investigated promptly and fairly.
26. Disciplinary Action: Consequences for violations, ranging from a warning or mandatory training to suspension or termination of employment.
27. Health and Safety: Employees must follow all safety guidelines to ensure a secure work environment (both in-office and at home).
28. Public Statements: Only authorized spokespersons may speak to the media or public on behalf of the company.
29. Accurate Record-Keeping: All business records, timesheets, and expense reports must be truthful and accurate.
30. Policy Acknowledgement: All employees must read, understand, and sign an acknowledgement of this Code of Conduct annually.
