SECURITY GUIDELINES

Your Responsibility: You are the first line of defense for company security.
Password Policy (Length): All passwords must be a minimum of 14 characters.
Password Policy (Complexity): Must include uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.
Password Uniqueness: Never reuse passwords across different systems.
Password Manager: Use the company-provided password manager (e.g., 1Password) to store all credentials.
Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): MFA is mandatory on all accounts that support it (SSO, email, code repos).
Phishing Awareness: Be suspicious of any email demanding urgent action, asking for credentials, or containing unexpected attachments.
Reporting Phishing: If you receive a suspicious email, use the "Report Phishing" button in Outlook or forward it to security@company.com.
Clicking Links: Do not click links or download attachments from unknown or unverified senders.
Data Classification (Public): Data intended for public consumption (e.g., marketing materials, press releases).
Data Classification (Internal): Data for internal company use, not highly sensitive (e.g., team-wide documents).
Data Classification (Confidential): Sensitive data that must be protected (e.g., client lists, project code, financial reports).
Data Classification (Restricted): Highly sensitive data (e.g., PII, passwords, HR records) with a strict "need-to-know" access.
Data Handling: Never email Confidential or Restricted data to personal accounts or upload it to unapproved cloud services.
Acceptable Use: Company equipment (laptops, phones) is for business use; incidental personal use is permitted but monitored.
Prohibited Software: Do not install unauthorized, unlicensed, or "cracked" software on your company laptop.
Software Installation: All new software must be requested through the IT Software Portal.
Public Wi-Fi: Avoid using public, unsecured Wi-Fi (e.g., cafes, airports). If you must, ensure the VPN is always connected.
VPN Usage: The VPN must be connected whenever you are working remotely or outside the office.
Physical Security (Office): Always wear your ID badge; do not tailgate or let others follow you into the building.
Physical Security (Visitors): All visitors must be registered and escorted by an employee.
Clean Desk Policy: Lock your screen (Win+L / Cmd+Ctrl+Q) every time you step away from your desk.
End-of-Day Security: Secure all sensitive documents in locked cabinets; do not leave laptops in the office overnight.
Remote Work Security: Ensure your home Wi-Fi network is secured with a strong WPA2/WPA3 password.
Device Security: Never leave your company laptop unattended in a public place (e.g., car, coffee shop).
Device Loss/Theft: Report a lost or stolen laptop or phone to the IT Service Desk immediately (within 1 hour).
USB / Removable Media: Do not plug in unknown USB drives; use of removable media is generally restricted.
Social Engineering: Be wary of attempts to gain information over the phone or via chat (e.g., someone pretending to be from IT).
Incident Reporting: Report any security weakness, suspected breach, or policy violation to security@company.com immediately.
Annual Training: All employees must complete the mandatory security awareness training once per year.
